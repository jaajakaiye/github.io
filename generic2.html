GPA vs. SAT

Project 2

The basis for this project is, "would a student's SAT score determines their graduating GPA in College." The data analysis in this project would explain this outcome. I used the Linear Regression analysis because it's a linear approximation of a casual relationship between two or more variables. Linear Regression is probably the most fundamental machine learning method out there and starting point for the advanced analytical learning path. This method would predict GPA based on the SAT scores obtained from student's standardized testing.

SAT is known as "standardized testing" students sit for as Seniors in High School to apply to Universities in the United States. It measures the student's ability in Mathematics, Critical Reading, and Writing. The higher the score, the higher the chance the student would be admitted to their top choice University. The SAT is based on a 1600*-point scale; there is no penalty for wrong answers, so the raw score is the sum of the number of questions you answer correctly.

*In March 2016, the SAT underwent a massive redesign, part of which included a change to its scoring system: it shifted from a 2400-point scale to a 1600-point scale.

GPA is known as "grade point average," which indicates how well or high students scored in their courses on average. Usually, the GPA scale is between "1.0 to 4.0" and shows the level of success during their four years in college.

The process applying to Universities begins with the following:

  • Sit for SAT
  • Get SAT score
  • Apply to University
  • Attend University
  • Graduate with GPA

    • As I stated before,"would a student's SAT score determines their graduating GPA in College;" why would I predict GPA with SAT? The reason is SAT is considered one of the best estimators of intellectual capacity and capability. Almost all colleges across the USA are using the SAT as a proxy for admission. The SAT has stood the test of time since it was debut in 1926 and administered by the College Board.
