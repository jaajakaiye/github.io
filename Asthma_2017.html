Introduction

Allegheny County (Pennsylvania) and the City of Pittsburgh both publish their data through the Western Pennsylvania Regional Data Center. In addition, the Western Pennsylvania Regional Data Center supports key community initiatives by making public information easier to find and use.

The Data Center maintains Allegheny County and the City of Pittsburgh’s open data portal and provides several services to data publishers and users. The Data Center also hosts datasets from these public sector agencies, academic institutions, and non-profit organizations. The University of Pittsburgh’s Center for Social and Urban Research manages the Data Center and collaborates with the University, Allegheny County, and Pittsburgh’s City.

Metadata Updated: November 30, 2020

This data shows healthcare utilization for asthma by Allegheny County and City of Pittsburg residents. It counts asthma-related visits to the Emergency Department (ED), hospitalizations, urgent care visits, and asthma controller medication dispensing events.

The asthma data was compiled as part of the Allegheny County Health Department’s Asthma Task Force, established in 2018. The Task Force was formed to identify strategies to decrease asthma inpatient and emergency utilization among children (ages 0-18), focusing on children receiving services funded by Medicaid. With the increase of asthma-related usage in Allegheny County, the data is being used to understand and gather data to improve and inform the task force’s recommended actions. In addition, this data will also be used to evaluate progress toward reducing asthma-related hospitalization and ED visits. The Allegheny County Health Department’s Asthma Task Force criteria of asthma utilization are:

  • at least 1 ED visit (dx 1-10 J45.xxx)
  • at least 1 acute inpatient encounter (dx 1-10 J45.xxx)
  • at least 1 outpatient visit or observational stay (dx 1-10 J45.xxx)
  • at least 1 asthma controller medication dispensing event

Regarding this data, we defined asthma using the International Classification of Diseases, Tenth Revision (IDC-10) classification system code J45.xxx here. The ICD-10 system classifies diagnoses, symptoms, and procedures in the U.S. healthcare system.

Children seeking care for an asthma-related claim in 2017 are represented in the data. The Health Department compiles data from medical claims submitted to three health plans (UPMC, Gateway Health, and Highmark). Claims may also come from people enrolled in Medicaid plans managed by these insurers. The Health Department estimates that 74% of the County’s population aged 0-18 is represented in the data.

The Data Center Objective

The objective is to decrease asthma inpatient and emergency utilization among children (ages 0-18) and evaluate progress toward reducing asthma-related hospitalization and Emergency department visits.

From this dataframe, I’ll create another dataframe combining ed_visits and ed_hosp since uc_visits is urgent care. The business task is to decrease asthma utilization in children under 18 admitted to the Emergency Department.

         uc_visits,

Summary of my analysis

The Data Center’s objective is to reduce children 18 years and younger from being admitted to the Emergency Department and asthma utilization usage in Allegheny County and the City of Pittsburgh.

The analysis of the Data Center illustrates children under 18 in Allegheny County, and the City of Pittsburgh shows an increase in asthma utilization; however, a decrease in visitations and hospitalizations.

I would recommend the Data Center to create an ID for the residents under 18 in gauging if the same residents are visiting or hospitalized multiple times in the year. Unfortunately, there’s not enough data to analyze if residents are counted once or numerous times based on the Health Department’s Asthma Task Force criteria.
